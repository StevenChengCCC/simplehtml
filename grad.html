Graduate Focus: Inclusive Product + Neurodiversity

This page satisfies the “graduate students must include an additional page” requirement.

Projected Career Path

My goal is to pursue product management focused on inclusive and accessible consumer products, especially features that reduce cognitive load and make complex platforms easier to navigate for neurodivergent users.

Experience to Date

Two projects that anchor my graduate focus are SafeBoard and my Social Media + Autism research work.

SafeBoard Prototype (safer, lower-stimulation feed)

  • Designed a “safe mode” social feed concept that emphasizes predictability and user control (content warnings, pacing, and clear UI states).
  • Explored ways to reduce sensory overload (less visual noise, fewer competing elements, optional media reveal).
  • Focused on plain-language explanations so users can understand what actions do before committing.

Social Media + Autism (research → product artifact)

  • Synthesized research insights about how autistic and cross-cultural users experience online platforms (e.g., unclear norms, ambiguous language, and rapid content changes).
  • Translated findings into actionable UX patterns (slang explanations, content controls, and step-by-step safety guidance).
  • Implemented these ideas in Social Media Academy as a practical learning site and prototype.

Anticipated Contributions

  • Inclusive product patterns: Ship features that improve clarity, reduce cognitive load, and provide safer defaults—without reducing capability for power users.
  • Research-to-build workflow: Turn qualitative insights into prototypes quickly, then validate with lightweight experiments and metrics.
  • Responsible engagement design: Design content controls and recommendation surfaces that support user intent, not just time-on-platform.
