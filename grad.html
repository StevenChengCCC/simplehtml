Graduate Focus: Inclusive Product & Accessibility

I’m pursuing work at the intersection of product development and accessibility—building tools that are usable, respectful, and genuinely helpful for diverse users.

Projected career path

Product Management / Product Development focused on accessible, research‑informed experiences—especially where software supports learning, communication, or safety in online communities.

  • Short term: build and ship user-facing products; strengthen metrics and experimentation practice.
  • Mid term: lead inclusive product initiatives; connect research insights to scalable product decisions.
  • Long term: drive accessibility as a core product principle (not a compliance checkbox).

What I bring to the field

  • Hands-on experience translating research into features and prototypes.
  • Comfort working across qualitative + quantitative evidence (interviews, behavioral data, metrics).
  • Engineering background to reduce “handoff loss” between idea → implementation → deployment.

Experience to date

SafeBoard

A concept/prototype direction focused on helping users regulate and navigate online spaces with lower cognitive load. The goal is to make “safe choices” more obvious through clear UI patterns, gentle content gating, and user-controlled disclosure.

  • Emphasis on clarity: fewer simultaneous options, predictable navigation, and reduced visual noise.
  • Respectful safety: users control what they see and when (instead of forcing exposure).
  • Prototype mindset: iterate quickly, test with users, and refine based on observed friction.

Social Media + Autism

Research and product exploration around how people engage with social media, and how design decisions can support accessibility and reduce harmful interactions—especially for neurodivergent users.

  • Combine behavioral patterns with qualitative insights to understand real user needs.
  • Design for comprehension: explain slang/context, reduce ambiguity, and support safer decision-making.
  • Bridge research → product: turn themes into concrete UI/feature recommendations.

Anticipated contributions

I want to help teams build products that are inclusive by default. My contribution is not only “building features,” but making sure features are understandable, measurable, and aligned with real user outcomes—especially for users who are often underserved by mainstream design.
